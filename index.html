I’m a PhD student working at the intersection of machine learning and environmental sustainability. My current research is on wildfire resilience, and more broadly I build multimodal and geospatial AI to help communities and the ecosystems they depend on become more resilient. Research

My PhD research is on wildfire resilience. I build graph neural network models that capture how wildfire risk and its impacts spread across connected landscapes, infrastructure, and communities, so they can be anticipated and planned for.

More broadly, my work sits at the intersection of machine learning and environmental sustainability, developing data-driven approaches to assess and mitigate environmental risk. I use machine learning and statistical inference to model environmental and infrastructure systems, often with multimodal and geospatial data. My interests span climate resilience, sustainable resource management, energy and water systems, climate risk forecasting and adaptation, and responsible, sustainable AI that can inform policy and large-scale sustainability efforts. Running through all of it is a focus on equitable disaster recovery, so the models guiding relief and rebuilding don’t overlook the people & ecosystems who need help most.
